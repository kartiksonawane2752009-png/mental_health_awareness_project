What is Mental Health?

A complete guide to understanding mental well-being — what it means, what affects it, and why it matters for every single person.

Definition

More than the absence of illness

Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It influences how we think, feel, and behave in daily life. It also shapes how we handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions.

Good mental health is not simply the absence of mental illness. It is a state of well-being that allows a person to live productively and cope with everyday challenges.

Mental health is important at every stage of life — from childhood and adolescence through adulthood and old age.

WHO Definition

"Mental health is a state of well-being in which an individual realizes their own abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work productively, and is able to contribute to their community."
— World Health Organization (WHO)
The Three Pillars

What mental health includes

Mental health is made up of three interconnected dimensions that work together.

💛

Emotional

The ability to understand, express, and manage your feelings in healthy ways — including coping with sadness, anger, and joy.

🧩

Psychological

How we think about ourselves and the world — includes self-esteem, sense of purpose, ability to concentrate, and resilience.

🌐

Social

Our ability to form meaningful relationships, contribute to communities, and feel a sense of belonging with others.

Influencing Factors

What affects mental health?

Mental health is shaped by a mix of biological, personal, and environmental factors.

🔬 Biological Factors

  • Brain chemistry and genetics
  • Hormonal changes (puberty, pregnancy)
  • Chronic physical illness
  • Sleep disorders and poor nutrition
  • Family history of mental illness

🌍 Environmental Factors

  • Childhood trauma or abuse
  • Academic or work-related pressure
  • Social isolation and loneliness
  • Poverty or financial instability
  • Discrimination or social injustice

🧠 Psychological Factors

  • Low self-esteem or negative self-talk
  • Unhealthy coping mechanisms
  • Difficulty managing emotions
  • Loss of a loved one
  • Lack of purpose or direction

🛡️ Protective Factors

  • Strong social support network
  • Access to mental health care
  • Sense of meaning and purpose
  • Healthy lifestyle habits
  • Resilience and problem-solving skills
