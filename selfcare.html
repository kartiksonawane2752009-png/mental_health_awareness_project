Self-Care Tips

Small habits, big difference

You don't need to overhaul your entire life. Small, consistent actions done regularly can meaningfully improve your mental health.

🗣️ 01

Talk to Someone

Sharing your feelings with a trusted person — friend, family, or counsellor — can lift a huge weight off your mind.

🏃 02

Stay Physically Active

Even a 30-minute walk releases endorphins — natural mood boosters. Exercise is one of the most effective free mental health tools.

📵 03

Limit Social Media

Constant comparison and negative news worsen anxiety. Set screen time limits and take regular digital detox days.

😴 04

Sleep & Eat Well

7–9 hours of sleep and a balanced diet are the biological foundation of mental health. Don't underestimate them.

🧘 05

Practice Mindfulness

Even 5–10 minutes of mindful breathing daily can calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety significantly.

📝 06

Journal Your Thoughts

Writing down worries, gratitude, or reflections helps process emotions and gain perspective. Just be honest.

🎨 07

Do What You Love

Hobbies — music, art, reading, sports — restore energy and provide joy. They are not luxuries; they are necessary.

🌿 08

Spend Time in Nature

Even 20 minutes outdoors measurably reduces stress hormones and improves mood, according to multiple studies.

👨‍⚕️ 09

Seek Professional Help

There is no shame in seeing a therapist or counsellor. Mental health professionals are trained to guide you.

Busting Stigma

Myths vs Facts

Misinformation creates stigma. Stigma stops people from seeking help. Let's replace the most harmful myths with facts.

❌ Myth

Mental health problems are a sign of personal weakness or a character flaw.

✅ Fact

Mental illness has biological, psychological, and social causes — just like physical illness. It has nothing to do with weakness.

❌ Myth

You can just "snap out of it" or "think positive" to overcome mental illness.

✅ Fact

Mental health conditions are medical conditions. Willpower alone is not a treatment — professional help matters.

❌ Myth

People with mental illness are dangerous, violent, or unpredictable.

✅ Fact

The vast majority of people with mental illness are not violent. They are far more likely to be victims of crime than perpetrators.

❌ Myth

Therapy is only for people who are "crazy" or severely ill.

✅ Fact

Therapy helps everyone — from managing daily stress to serious conditions. It's a proactive, healthy choice, not a last resort.

❌ Myth

Children and teenagers don't experience real mental health problems.

✅ Fact

Half of all mental health conditions begin by age 14. Young people experience real distress that deserves to be taken seriously.

❌ Myth

Once you have a mental illness, it's permanent and you'll never recover.

✅ Fact

With the right support and treatment, people recover from mental illness and live full, productive, meaningful lives.
