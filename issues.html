😔 Mood Disorder

Depression

More than just sadness — depression is a serious medical condition affecting how a person feels, thinks, and handles daily life.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in daily activities
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating

Root Causes

  • Brain chemistry imbalances
  • Family history of depression
  • Traumatic or stressful life events
  • Chronic physical illness
  • Social isolation and loneliness

Treatment Options

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Antidepressant medications
  • Regular physical exercise
  • Social support and peer groups
  • Mindfulness and meditation

How to Help Someone

  • Listen without judgment
  • Encourage professional help gently
  • Check in regularly
  • Avoid saying "just cheer up"
  • Help with small daily tasks
😰 Anxiety Disorder

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety is a normal emotion, but anxiety disorders involve excessive, persistent fear or worry that interferes with daily life.

Types of Anxiety

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
  • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Panic Disorder
  • Specific Phobias
  • Separation Anxiety

Physical Symptoms

  • Racing or pounding heartbeat
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating and trembling
  • Nausea or stomach issues
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness

Psychological Symptoms

  • Constant, uncontrollable worry
  • Fear of the worst happening
  • Difficulty relaxing or sleeping
  • Irritability and restlessness
  • Avoidance of triggering situations

Management Strategies

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Deep breathing and grounding
  • Limiting caffeine and alcohol
  • Regular exercise routine
  • Anti-anxiety medication if needed
💤 Common Condition

Stress & Burnout

Chronic stress from academic or work environments can lead to burnout — complete emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion.

Signs of Burnout

  • Constant exhaustion even after sleep
  • Feeling detached or cynical
  • Reduced performance and motivation
  • Frequent headaches or body aches
  • Emotional numbness

Common Triggers

  • Academic or work overload
  • Lack of control over situations
  • Poor study-life balance
  • Unclear expectations
  • Toxic environments

Recovery Steps

  • Take intentional breaks and rest
  • Set clear boundaries on study time
  • Talk to a counsellor or mentor
  • Focus on energy-restoring activities
  • Prioritize sleep and nutrition

Prevention Tips

  • Learn to say no without guilt
  • Delegate tasks when possible
  • Schedule regular "off" time
  • Build a support network
  • Recognize early warning signs
🔄 OCD

OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)

Unwanted recurring thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions) performed to ease the distress they cause.

Common Obsessions

  • Fear of contamination or germs
  • Fear of harming yourself or others
  • Need for symmetry or exactness
  • Intrusive, disturbing thoughts
  • Fear of losing important things

Common Compulsions

  • Excessive handwashing or cleaning
  • Checking locks or appliances repeatedly
  • Counting, tapping, arranging objects
  • Seeking reassurance constantly
  • Mental rituals like repeating words

Treatment

  • Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
  • SSRI medications
  • Support groups
  • Mindfulness-based approaches

Important Notes

  • OCD is NOT a personality trait
  • People know their fears are irrational
  • It can be managed with treatment
  • Early help leads to better outcomes
  • OCD does not define who you are
⚡ Mood Disorder

Bipolar Disorder

Extreme shifts between emotional highs (mania) and lows (depression), often with stable periods in between.

Manic Episode Signs

  • Unusually high energy or euphoria
  • Reduced need for sleep
  • Racing thoughts and rapid speech
  • Inflated self-esteem
  • Impulsive or risky decisions

Depressive Episode Signs

  • Deep sadness and hopelessness
  • Loss of energy and motivation
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Changes in sleep and appetite
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Types of Bipolar

  • Bipolar I — full manic episodes
  • Bipolar II — hypomania + depression
  • Cyclothymia — milder but chronic

Treatment

  • Mood stabilizers (lithium etc.)
  • Psychotherapy and family therapy
  • Regular sleep and routine
  • Avoiding alcohol and triggers
  • Ongoing psychiatric monitoring
🌩️ Trauma-Related

PTSD

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder can develop after experiencing trauma. It is not a sign of weakness — it is the mind's response to overwhelming stress.

Common Triggers

  • Physical or sexual abuse
  • Accidents or natural disasters
  • War or violence
  • Childhood neglect or trauma
  • Sudden loss of a loved one

Symptoms

  • Flashbacks and intrusive memories
  • Nightmares about the event
  • Emotional numbness or detachment
  • Always feeling "on guard"
  • Avoiding people or reminders

Treatments

  • EMDR therapy
  • Trauma-focused CBT
  • Prolonged Exposure Therapy
  • Medication for anxiety/depression
  • Safe, structured support groups

Supporting Someone

  • Never force them to talk about trauma
  • Be patient — healing is not linear
  • Learn and respect their triggers
  • Encourage professional help gently
  • Take care of your own well-being too
